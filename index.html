Welcome to the Tarot Guide

The tarot is a collection of 78 cards, each with its own imagery, symbolism, and story. It's often used as a tool for self-reflection and guidance, helping us to gain new perspectives on our lives and the world around us.

The deck is split into two main sections:

  • The Major Arcana (22 cards): These are the trump cards that represent major life lessons, karmic influences, and significant milestones on a spiritual journey.
  • The Minor Arcana (56 cards): These are organized into four suits‚ÄîWands, Cups, Swords, and Pentacles‚Äîand represent the day-to-day challenges, emotions, and practicalities of life.

The Major Arcana

These 22 cards represent major life lessons and karmic influences. They are the heart of the tarot deck. Click on a card to learn more.

The Suit of Wands

The Suit of Wands is associated with the element of Fire and represents creativity, passion, ambition, and business.

The Suit of Cups

The Suit of Cups is associated with the element of Water and represents emotions, intuition, relationships, and love.

The Suit of Swords

The Suit of Swords is associated with the element of Air and represents intellect, logic, truth, and challenges.

The Suit of Pentacles

The Suit of Pentacles is associated with the element of Earth and represents the material world, finances, career, and physical health.

Common Spreads

Tarot spreads are arrangements of cards that offer a structured way to answer a question or gain insight into a situation.
